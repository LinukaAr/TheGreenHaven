Human-Wildlife Conflict

In the heart of Sri Lanka, the age-old coexistence between humans and elephants is fracturing. As the woods are cut down, farmers’ plots are established at random and huge development plans encroach on forests where elephants live, these great animals begin leaving their ancient homes for people’s towns. As such there is a lot of human-elephant conflict with terrified farmers fighting back for their food and lives leading to fatalities from both sides. Nevertheless, conservationists have been working towards new methods of ensuring coexistence is more harmonious. This article looks at the conflict escalation and attempts made towards sustainable answers in Sri Lanka.

Causes and Consequences

Human-wildlife conflict arises due to various factors such as habitat loss, competition for resources, and human encroachment into wildlife territories...

Consequences include loss of life, injury to both humans and animals, and economic losses for communities dependent on agriculture and livestock...

Conflict Mitigation Strategies

Effective strategies for mitigating human-wildlife conflict involve community engagement, use of technology, and creating buffer zones...

Examples include setting up wildlife corridors, using non-lethal deterrents, and promoting sustainable land-use practices...

Success Stories of Coexistence

There are numerous success stories where communities have learned to coexist with wildlife, benefiting both humans and animals...

For instance, community-based conservation projects in Africa and Asia have shown positive outcomes in reducing conflict and promoting wildlife conservation...
